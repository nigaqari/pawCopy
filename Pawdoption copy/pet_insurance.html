Frequently Asked Questions

Pet insurance typically covers unexpected veterinary costs, including accidents, illnesses, and emergency treatments. Some plans may also include routine care, such as vaccinations, dental cleaning, and preventive treatments, if purchased as add-ons.

Pet insurance usually operates on a reimbursement model. You pay the vet directly for treatment and then submit a claim to the insurance provider. Depending on your policy, you'll be reimbursed for a percentage of the cost (e.g., 70%, 80%, or 90%) after deductibles are applied

Most pet insurance policies do not cover pre-existing conditions, which are health issues your pet had before the insurance policy started. However, some providers may cover conditions that are considered curable after a waiting period. Let me know if you need additional questions or if these need adjusting!
